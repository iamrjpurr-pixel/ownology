# Boutique Winery — Minimum Equipment & Tools Brainstorm
*Organised from shed roof to floor. Assumes a production scale of 5–50 tonnes of fruit per vintage.*

---

## 1. BUILDING SHELL & INFRASTRUCTURE (Roof to Walls to Floor)

### 1.1 Roof & Structure
| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Insulated roof sheeting (Colorbond or equivalent) | Thermal mass control is critical — fermentation and maturation are temperature-sensitive |
| Roof ventilation / ridge vents | Removes CO₂ build-up during fermentation (safety requirement) |
| Guttering and rainwater diversion | Effluent management — winery wastewater must not mix with clean stormwater |
| Skylights or high-set windows | Natural light reduces electrical load; UV-filtering glass protects wine in tanks |
| Bird and vermin proofing | Food safety compliance |

### 1.2 Walls & Doors
| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Insulated wall panels or cavity brick | Temperature stability for maturation area |
| Wide roller door (min 3.5 m) | Forklift and pallet access for barrels, bins, and equipment |
| Pest-proof seals on all penetrations | Food safety / biosecurity |
| Washable wall surfaces in production areas | FSANZ food safety standard |

### 1.3 Electrical
| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Three-phase power supply | Required for crusher-destemmer, press, pumps, refrigeration |
| Weatherproof GPOs at multiple heights | Pumps, lab equipment, lighting |
| Emergency stop buttons near all major equipment | WHS requirement |
| Explosion-proof fittings in CO₂-risk zones | WHS / AS/NZS 3000 compliance |
| Backup generator connection point | Protects ferments during power outages |

### 1.4 Plumbing & Water
| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Hot and cold water supply (min 40 L/min) | Cleaning-in-place (CIP), tank washing |
| Dedicated winery water meter | Required for water licence compliance in most states |
| Backflow prevention device | Protects potable water supply |
| High-pressure wash-down hose reels | Minimum 2 — one per production zone |
| Hot water unit (min 80 L, 80°C capable) | Sanitising tanks and equipment |

### 1.5 Drainage & Effluent
| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Epoxy-coated concrete floor with 1:80 fall to drains | Prevents pooling; food-safe surface |
| Stainless steel or polymer slot drains (min 150 mm wide) | Handles grape marc, lees, and wash water |
| Grease/solids trap | Captures marc and lees before effluent system |
| Effluent holding tank or wetland system | Winery wastewater is high-BOD — cannot go to stormwater or sewer without treatment |
| Separate stormwater and process water drainage | Required by most state EPAs |

### 1.6 Refrigeration Infrastructure
| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Glycol chiller (min 10 kW for 10-tonne operation) | Fermentation temperature control and cold stabilisation |
| Insulated glycol pipework to all tanks | Stainless or food-grade poly |
| Refrigerated maturation room or cool room | Target 12–15°C year-round for barrel storage |
| Temperature monitoring system (min 1 probe per zone) | Data logging preferred for quality records |

## 2. RECEIVAL & GRAPE PROCESSING

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Grape receival bin / hopper (1–2 tonne) | Stainless or food-grade poly; gravity or forklift-fed |
| Forklift or front-end loader | Moves bins; 2-tonne capacity minimum |
| Platform scales (min 2-tonne capacity) | Weighing fruit for records and WET compliance |
| Crusher-destemmer (electric, 5–20 t/hr) | Core piece of equipment; stainless rollers preferred |
| Sorting table (optional but recommended) | Hand-sorting before crush for quality control |
| Peristaltic or centrifugal must pump | Moves crushed must to fermenters |
| Sulphur dioxide (SO₂) dosing equipment | Inline SO₂ addition at receival |
| Refrigerated must chiller (optional) | Cold soak for red wines; skin contact for whites |

---

## 3. PRESSING

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Pneumatic basket or membrane press (0.5–2 tonne capacity) | Membrane press preferred for quality; basket press is lower cost |
| Press pan / drip tray | Catches free-run juice |
| Juice/must collection vessel (food-grade) | Stainless tank or food-grade poly bin |
| Press cloths (basket press only) | Consumable; multiple sets required |

---

## 4. FERMENTATION

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Open-top fermenters — red wine (stainless or food-grade poly, 1–5 tonne) | Minimum 2–3 vessels; allows cap management |
| Closed-top tanks — white/rosé (stainless, variable capacity) | Minimum 2 vessels with glycol jackets |
| Fermentation airlocks and bungs | Consumable; multiple sets |
| Plunger / punch-down tool (manual or pneumatic) | Cap management for red ferments |
| Pump-over hose and fittings | Pump-over for extraction and aeration |
| Inoculation equipment (hydration vessel, thermometer) | Yeast and bacteria rehydration |
| Brix/density measurement (hydrometer or electronic) | Daily fermentation monitoring |
| Temperature probes (one per fermenter) | Glycol control feedback |
| CO₂ warning detector | Safety — CO₂ is heavier than air and accumulates at floor level |

---

## 5. MATURATION & STORAGE

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Oak barrels (225 L French or American, new to 3-year) | Minimum 4–6 for a 5-tonne operation |
| Stainless steel maturation tanks (variable capacity) | For non-oak whites and rosé |
| Barrel racks (A-frame or single-stack) | Timber or steel; rated for 300 kg per barrel |
| Barrel bung and bung mallet | Consumable |
| Barrel thief / wine thief | Sampling without significant oxygen exposure |
| Topping wine vessel (5–20 L) | Prevents ullage during maturation |
| Inert gas (nitrogen or argon) system | Blanketing tanks and barrels |
| Barrel washer (steam or hot water) | Cleaning and sanitising between vintages |

## 6. LABORATORY & ANALYSIS

*The minimum viable lab for a boutique winery. Larger operations should consider a discrete analyser.*

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| **pH meter** (calibrated, 0.01 resolution) | Essential — used at every stage |
| **Titration burette and stand** | Free SO₂ and TA (titratable acidity) measurement |
| **Ripper SO₂ kit** (iodine titration) | Free and total SO₂ monitoring |
| **Refractometer** (Brix) | Harvest and fermentation monitoring |
| **Hydrometer set** (Brix and alcohol) | Fermentation progress and final alcohol check |
| **Analytical balance** (0.01 g resolution) | Weighing additions (SO₂, nutrients, fining agents) |
| **Graduated cylinders and pipettes** | 10 mL, 50 mL, 100 mL, 500 mL |
| **Magnetic stirrer** | Dissolving additions and titration |
| **Ebulliometer or distillation unit** | Alcohol determination |
| **Turbidity meter (NTU)** | Juice and wine clarity assessment |
| **Microscope** (optional but recommended) | Yeast and bacteria identification |
| **Bench centrifuge** (optional) | Juice settling and lees analysis |
| **Chemical reagents** | H₂SO₄, NaOH, iodine, starch indicator, PVPP, bentonite |
| **Lab notebook / LIMS** | Record-keeping for regulatory compliance |
| **Calibration standards** | pH buffers (4.0, 7.0), conductivity standards |

## 7. ADDITIONS & WINEMAKING INPUTS

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| SO₂ stock solution (10% w/v) or potassium metabisulphite | Primary preservative |
| Tartaric acid | TA adjustment |
| Calcium carbonate | Acid reduction (deacidification) |
| Diammonium phosphate (DAP) | Yeast assimilable nitrogen (YAN) supplement |
| Commercial yeast strains (dry or liquid) | Multiple strains for different styles |
| Malolactic bacteria (freeze-dried) | MLF inoculation |
| Bentonite | Protein stabilisation (whites) |
| Fining agents (egg white, isinglass, PVPP, casein) | Style-dependent |
| Copper sulphate solution | H₂S removal |
| Oak alternatives (chips, staves, spirals) | Cost-effective oak influence |
| Nitrogen gas cylinders | Inert blanketing |
| Citric acid (cleaning) | Descaling |
| Caustic soda / sodium hydroxide (cleaning) | CIP cleaning |
| Peracetic acid or sodium percarbonate (sanitising) | Tank and barrel sanitising |

---

## 8. FILTRATION & STABILISATION

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Plate-and-frame filter (pad filter) | Coarse to sterile filtration; minimum 20-plate for boutique scale |
| Filter pads (coarse, medium, fine, sterile) | Consumable; multiple grades |
| Diatomaceous earth (DE) filter (optional) | Higher throughput for lees-heavy wines |
| Bentonite addition and settling tank | Cold stabilisation for whites |
| Cold stabilisation tank (glycol-jacketed) | Tartrate precipitation; hold at −4°C for 7–10 days |
| In-line filter housing (0.45 µm membrane) | Pre-bottling sterile filtration |

---

## 9. PACKAGING & BOTTLING

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Bottle rinser / inverter | Removes dust and debris before filling |
| Gravity or semi-automatic bottle filler (4–8 head) | Minimum for boutique scale; inert gas purge preferred |
| Corker (floor-standing, semi-automatic) | Cork or DIAM closures |
| Screw-cap closure machine (optional) | Stelvin or equivalent |
| Capsule spinner / heat-shrink applicator | Foil or PVC capsules |
| Label applicator (manual or semi-auto) | Front and back labels |
| Bottle conveyor / bench | Stainless or food-grade surface |
| Case erector and tape gun | Cardboard case packing |
| Pallet jack (manual) | Moving finished goods |
| Finished goods storage racking | Minimum 2°C–18°C stable environment |

---

## 10. SAFETY & COMPLIANCE EQUIPMENT

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| CO₂ gas detector (fixed, with alarm) | Mandatory in enclosed fermentation areas |
| Fire extinguishers (CO₂ and dry powder) | One per 200 m² minimum |
| Eye wash station | Chemical handling areas |
| First aid kit (Class C) | Workplace requirement |
| Chemical storage cabinet (flammable) | Ethanol, cleaning chemicals |
| SDS (Safety Data Sheets) folder | All chemicals on site |
| PPE: gloves, goggles, apron, gumboots | Per person working in production |
| Confined space entry kit | Tanks are confined spaces under WHS legislation |
| Spill containment bunding | Around chemical storage and tank farm |
| Signage: CO₂ hazard, confined space, PPE required | WHS obligation |

---

## 11. RECORD-KEEPING & COMPLIANCE TOOLS

| Item | Notes |
|---|---|
| Vintage record book / winemaking log | Required for Label Integrity Program (LIP) compliance |
| Weighbridge dockets / fruit receival records | WET and LIP compliance |
| Chemical addition log | Tracks SO₂, acid, nutrients per batch |
| Tank movement register | Traces wine from fruit to bottle |
| Cellar door sales register | Required for liquor licence compliance |
| AWBC/Wine Australia registration | Mandatory for all wine producers |
| Calibration log for lab instruments | Best practice; required if exporting |

---

## SUMMARY — Minimum Viable Boutique Winery (5–10 Tonne)

| Zone | Non-Negotiable Items |
|---|---|
| Building | 3-phase power, glycol chiller, slot drains, effluent system, CO₂ ventilation |
| Processing | Crusher-destemmer, pump, press, receival bin, scales |
| Fermentation | 2× open-top red fermenters, 2× closed-top white tanks, glycol control |
| Maturation | 4–6 barrels, 2× stainless tanks, inert gas |
| Lab | pH meter, SO₂ titration kit, refractometer, hydrometer, balance |
| Packaging | Bottle filler, corker, labeller |
| Safety | CO₂ detector, eye wash, confined space kit, SDS folder |
| Compliance | Vintage log, fruit receival records, Wine Australia registration |

*Sources: Grapeworks Australia, AWRI Code of Good Manufacturing Practice, Wine Australia Compliance Guide, WHS Act 2011, FSANZ Standard 4.5.1*
