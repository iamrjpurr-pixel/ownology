# New South Wales — Winery Regulatory Requirements

> Compiled for boutique winery operators in New South Wales, Australia.
> Last updated: May 2026. Always verify current requirements with the relevant authority.

---

## 1. Liquor Licensing — Liquor & Gaming NSW

### Governing Legislation
**Liquor Act 2007 (NSW)**

### Licence Type: Producer/Wholesaler Licence
The Producer/Wholesaler Licence is the standard licence for NSW wineries. It is administered by **Liquor & Gaming NSW**, a division of the Department of Creative Industries, Tourism, Hospitality and Sport.

**What it authorises (for producers):**
- Selling wine (and other alcohol you produce) to both retail (general public) and wholesale customers
- Selling your alcohol for on-premises consumption (cellar door tastings) or takeaway
- Home delivery (phone, email, online or app orders)
- Selling at industry liquor shows, producers' markets and fairs
- Tastings on licensed premises

**Key conditions:**
- The licence holder and all staff involved in selling, serving or supplying alcohol must hold a current **Responsible Service of Alcohol (RSA)** certificate
- A public notice must be displayed after lodging an application; neighbours must be notified
- The application is published on the Liquor & Gaming NSW Noticeboard for **30 days** during which objections may be lodged
- Multiple premises may be covered under one licence in certain circumstances

**Trading hours (standard):**
- Monday to Saturday: 5 am to midnight
- Sunday: 10 am to 10 pm
- Extended trading hours may be applied for

**Application process:**
1. Apply online via the Liquor & Gaming NSW portal
2. Notify neighbours of your application
3. Application published on Noticeboard for 30 days
4. Liquor & Gaming NSW reviews and may request further information

**Contact:** Liquor & Gaming NSW — Phone: 1300 024 720 (Mon–Fri 9am–4pm)
**Email:** contact.us@liquorandgaming.nsw.gov.au
**Website:** [www.nsw.gov.au/liquorandgaming](https://www.nsw.gov.au/liquorandgaming)

## 2. Environmental Regulation — NSW EPA

### Governing Legislation
**Protection of the Environment Operations Act 1997 (NSW)** (POEO Act)

### Environment Protection Licence (EPL)
Under Schedule 1 of the POEO Act, certain industrial activities require an **Environment Protection Licence (EPL)** from the NSW EPA. Beverage manufacturing (including wineries) is a scheduled activity under the POEO Act.

**Boutique winery threshold:**
An EPL is generally required for food and beverage manufacturing operations that exceed the production thresholds specified in Schedule 1 of the POEO Act. **Small boutique wineries** producing below these thresholds typically do not require an EPL, but must still comply with general environmental duties under the POEO Act.

**General environmental duties (all wineries):**
- Must not pollute land, water or air
- Must not discharge winery wastewater (marc, lees, wash water) to waterways, stormwater drains or public sewers without authorisation
- Must manage winery wastewater through land application, evaporation ponds, or licensed trade waste agreements with the local water utility
- Must immediately notify the EPA and local authority of any pollution incident that causes or threatens material harm to the environment (POEO Act, Part 5.7)

**Wastewater management:**
- Land application of diluted winery wastewater is the most common approach for boutique wineries
- A **trade waste agreement** with the local water utility (e.g., Hunter Water, Sydney Water, Essential Water) is required if discharging to sewer
- Wastewater must be characterised (BOD, pH, sodium, chloride) before land application
- NSW DPI has published interim codes of practice for winery wastewater management (Hunter Valley region)

**Contact:** NSW EPA — 131 555 — [www.epa.nsw.gov.au](https://www.epa.nsw.gov.au)
**EPL applications:** eConnect EPA portal — [econnect.epa.nsw.gov.au](https://econnect.epa.nsw.gov.au)

## 3. Workplace Health and Safety — SafeWork NSW

### Governing Legislation
**Work Health and Safety Act 2011 (NSW)** and **Work Health and Safety Regulation 2017 (NSW)**

### Key Obligations
- Provide and maintain a working environment that is safe and without risks to health, so far as is reasonably practicable (SFARP)
- Identify and manage hazards: cellar equipment (pumps, presses, crushers), confined spaces (tanks, fermenters), chemicals (SO₂, caustic cleaning agents, CO₂ build-up during fermentation)
- Maintain plant and equipment to manufacturer specifications
- Provide adequate training, instruction and supervision
- Consult with workers on health and safety matters

### Specific Winery Hazards (SafeWork NSW Guide to Managing Risks in Wineries)
- **CO₂ accumulation** in fermentation areas — ventilation and atmospheric monitoring required; CO₂ can displace oxygen and cause rapid incapacitation
- **Confined space entry** (tanks, vats, pits) — confined space register, entry permit system, atmospheric testing, rescue procedures required
- **Manual handling** — crush/harvest season involves heavy lifting; risk assessments and mechanical aids required
- **Electrical safety** — wet cellar environments require appropriate IP-rated electrical installations; regular inspection required
- **Chemical handling** — SO₂ (sulfur dioxide), caustic cleaning agents, and tartaric acid require Safety Data Sheets (SDS), appropriate PPE, and staff training
- **Working at heights** — tank access platforms and ladders must comply with fall prevention requirements
- **Forklift and mobile plant** — traffic management plans required for harvest season

**Contact:** SafeWork NSW — 13 10 50 — [www.safework.nsw.gov.au](https://www.safework.nsw.gov.au)
**Reference:** *Guide to Managing Risks in Wineries* (SafeWork NSW, 2016)

## 4. Planning and Land Use — Local Council / Department of Planning

### Governing Legislation
**Environmental Planning and Assessment Act 1979 (NSW)** (EP&A Act)

### Development Applications
A **development application (DA)** to the relevant local council is required for:
- Establishing a new winery on rural or agricultural land
- Constructing or expanding cellar door facilities
- Operating a cellar door restaurant or function venue
- Significant changes to existing buildings or use

**Rural Zone (RU1/RU2) considerations:**
Most vineyard land is zoned RU1 (Primary Production) or RU2 (Rural Landscape). A winery is generally defined as a "rural industry" and requires development consent in these zones. Cellar door premises are defined separately in the Standard Instrument LEP and typically require consent.

**State Environmental Planning Policies (SEPPs):**
- **SEPP (Primary Production) 2021** may apply to winery development on rural land
- Local Environmental Plans (LEPs) and Development Control Plans (DCPs) vary by council — check with your local council

**Contact:** Your local council's planning department. NSW Planning Portal: [www.planningportal.nsw.gov.au](https://www.planningportal.nsw.gov.au)

## 5. Water Licensing — WaterNSW / NRAR

### Governing Legislation
**Water Management Act 2000 (NSW)**

### Water Access Licences
- A **water access licence** is required to extract water from a waterway, bore or aquifer for winery or vineyard use
- Licences are issued by the **Natural Resources Access Regulator (NRAR)** and managed through WaterNSW
- Water use must be metered and reported annually
- **Stormwater and wastewater** must not be discharged to waterways without EPA authorisation

**Contact:** NRAR — 1800 633 362 — [www.nrar.nsw.gov.au](https://www.nrar.nsw.gov.au)
WaterNSW — [www.waternsw.com.au](https://www.waternsw.com.au)

## 6. Food Safety — NSW Food Authority / Local Council

### Governing Legislation
**Food Act 2003 (NSW)** and **Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ) Food Standards Code**

### Notification Requirements
- Wineries that sell wine for consumption (including cellar door) are classified as **food businesses** under the Food Act 2003
- Most wineries are required to **notify** their local council of their food business activities (rather than hold a Food Authority licence)
- Compliance with the FSANZ Food Standards Code is mandatory (Standard 4.5 — Wine Production Requirements)
- Wineries that manufacture wine for wholesale supply may need to notify the NSW Food Authority directly

**Contact:** NSW Food Authority — 1300 552 406 — [www.foodauthority.nsw.gov.au](https://www.foodauthority.nsw.gov.au)
Local council environmental health officer for notification.

## 7. Federal Obligations (also applicable in NSW)

NSW winery operators must also comply with all federal regulatory requirements:

| Obligation | Authority | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Wine Equalisation Tax (WET) | ATO | Register if annual wine sales >$75,000; 29% WET applies |
| Label Integrity Program (LIP) | Wine Australia | Vintage, variety and region claims must be substantiated |
| Wine Australia registration | Wine Australia | Required for export; domestic producers should register |
| Excise (spirits only) | ATO | Excise manufacturer licence required if producing spirits |
| Biosecurity | DAFF | Phytosanitary requirements for vine material imports |

See the federal regulations document for full detail: `federal/australian-winery-federal-regulations.md`

## 8. Summary Checklist for a New Boutique Winery in NSW

| Step | Requirement | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Check local LEP/DCP; lodge development application | Local Council |
| 2 | Apply for Producer/Wholesaler Liquor Licence | Liquor & Gaming NSW |
| 3 | Confirm EPA EPL status (most boutique wineries below threshold) | NSW EPA |
| 4 | Arrange winery wastewater management (land application or trade waste agreement) | NSW EPA / Local Water Utility |
| 5 | Obtain water access licence | NRAR / WaterNSW |
| 6 | Notify local council as a food business | Local Council |
| 7 | Ensure WHS compliance (confined spaces, CO₂, chemicals, forklift) | SafeWork NSW |
| 8 | Register for WET with ATO (if sales >$75,000) | ATO |
| 9 | Register with Wine Australia | Wine Australia |
| 10 | Ensure all staff hold current RSA certificates | Liquor & Gaming NSW / Training Provider |
