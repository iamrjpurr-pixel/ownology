# Tasmania (TAS) — Winery Regulatory Reference

> Last updated: May 2026. Verify currency with the relevant authority before relying on this document.

## 1. Liquor Licensing

### 1.1 Producer's Licence (General Licence)
- **Legislation:** *Liquor Licensing Act 1990* (TAS)
- **Regulator:** Commissioner for Licensing, Department of Treasury and Finance
- **Requirement:** A liquor licence is required for any business that sells liquor on a regular basis, including winery cellar doors. A licence can only be granted to a person, not a company or club.
- **Licence types relevant to wineries:**
  - **General Licence** — allows sale of liquor between 5 am and midnight daily; typically issued to hotels and taverns but also used by some producers.
  - **Special Licence** — a licence subject to conditions; commonly used by wine producers, restaurants, and function centres.
  - **Off Licence** — for retail sales of liquor for consumption off the premises (e.g. cellar door bottle sales).
- **Application timeframe:** Aim for 4–6 weeks; some applications may take longer.
- **Contact:** licensing@treasury.tas.gov.au | (03) 6166 4040
- **URL:** https://www.treasury.tas.gov.au/liquor-and-gaming/liquor/licences

### 1.2 Small Producer's Permit
- **Legislation:** *Liquor Licensing Act 1990* (TAS)
- **Requirement:** Allows Tasmanian craft and boutique liquor producers to sell their liquor at events (markets, food festivals, agricultural shows). Granted for 12 months.
- **Eligibility (wine):** Total sales volume in the previous financial year < 28,500 litres; 85% of fruit must be grown in Tasmania; producer assumes financial risk for production.
- **New events:** Must obtain written approval from the Commissioner at least 7 days before the event.
- **Application:** Small Producers Permit Application form + application fee.
- **URL:** https://www.treasury.tas.gov.au/liquor-and-gaming/liquor/permits/small-producers-permit

## 2. Environmental Obligations

### 2.1 Environmental Management and Pollution Control Act 1994 (EMPCA)
- **Regulator:** Environment Protection Authority (EPA) Tasmania
- **Contact:** Enquiries@epa.tas.gov.au | (03) 6165 4599
- **URL:** https://epa.tas.gov.au

### 2.2 Level 2 Scheduled Activities (Environmental Licence Required)
Under EMPCA Schedule 2, a **Level 2 environmental licence** is required for:
- **Breweries and Distilleries:** Works with capacity to consume **100 kilolitres or more of water per 8-hour working day**.
- Note: Most boutique Tasmanian wineries fall below this threshold. Wineries are not separately listed in Schedule 2; however, wastewater discharge and chemical use obligations still apply under the general duty of care provisions of EMPCA.

### 2.3 Wastewater and Winery Effluent
- Winery wastewater (marc, lees, wash-down water) must be managed to prevent environmental harm under EMPCA.
- Discharge to waterways or land requires appropriate management — typically an effluent management system (land application, evaporation ponds, or licensed trade waste to council sewer).
- The VinØ Program (Wine Tasmania) provides best practice guidance on winery wastewater management.

### 2.4 Regulations Under EMPCA
- Environmental Management and Pollution Control (Smoke) Regulations 2019
- Environmental Management and Pollution Control (General) Regulations 2017
- Environmental Management and Pollution Control (Noise) Regulations 2016
- Environmental Management and Pollution Control (Waste Management) Regulations 2020

## 3. Work Health and Safety

### 3.1 Work Health and Safety Act 2012 (TAS)
- **Regulator:** WorkSafe Tasmania
- **Contact:** 1300 366 322 | worksafe.tas.gov.au
- **Requirement:** Duty of care to workers, contractors, and visitors. Winery-specific hazards include:
  - **Confined spaces:** Fermentation tanks, underground cellars, and storage areas can accumulate CO₂ to dangerous levels. A confined space entry procedure and atmospheric testing are required.
  - **Hazardous chemicals:** Sulfur dioxide (SO₂), caustic cleaning agents, and CO₂ are classified hazardous substances under the WHS Regulations.
  - **Manual handling:** Barrel handling, bin work, and harvest activities.
  - **Machinery:** Crusher-destemmers, presses, forklifts.
- **Incident notification:** Serious injuries, illnesses, and dangerous incidents must be notified to WorkSafe Tasmania immediately.
- **Codes of Practice:** Confined Spaces CoP, Managing Risks of Hazardous Chemicals CoP, and others available at worksafe.tas.gov.au/topics/laws-and-compliance/codes-of-practice

## 4. Planning and Land Use

### 4.1 Land Use Planning and Approvals Act 1993 (LUPAA)
- **Regulator:** Local Council (planning authority) + Tasmanian Planning Commission
- **Scheme:** Tasmanian Planning Scheme (TPS) — applies statewide from 2017.

### 4.2 Winery and Cellar Door Planning Classification
- A **winery** is classified as **Resource Processing** under the TPS.
- A **cellar door** (sale of farm goods/products) is classified as **Resource Processing** (associated with Resource Development).
- In the **Rural Zone** and **Agriculture Zone**, Resource Processing is a **Permitted** use — no discretionary assessment required for a standard winery + cellar door operation.
- Agritourism activities (wine tasting, cellar door sales, farm produce sales, cafes showcasing products) are Permitted if associated with Resource Processing in Rural/Agriculture zones.
- **Recommendation:** Discuss specific circumstances with a qualified planner or your local council before commencing.

### 4.3 State Policy on the Protection of Agricultural Land 2009
- Applies to all development in agricultural zones.
- Wineries must demonstrate compatibility with ongoing agricultural use of the land.

### 4.4 Primary Industry Activities Protection Act 1995
- Provides protection for primary producers (including viticulture) from nuisance claims related to normal farming activities (noise, dust, spray drift, odour).

## 5. Food Safety

### 5.1 Food Act 2003 (TAS) and Food Standards Code
- **Regulator:** Local Council (food business registration)
- **Requirement:** Any business selling food (including wine tastings with food, cellar door food service, or café at the winery) must be **registered as a food business** with the relevant local council.
- Registration is required before commencing operations.
- The Food Standards Code (FSANZ) prescribes permitted additives, processing aids, and labelling requirements for wine.
- **URL:** https://www.health.tas.gov.au (search "Starting a Food Business")

## 6. Water

### 6.1 Water Management Act 1999 (TAS)
- **Regulator:** Department of Natural Resources and Environment Tasmania (NRE Tas)
- **Requirement:** A **water licence** is required to take water from a surface water or groundwater source for irrigation (vineyard irrigation) or winery processing.
- Water licences are issued under a water management plan for the relevant catchment.
- Contact NRE Tas for licence applications and current allocation status.
- **URL:** https://nre.tas.gov.au/water

## 7. Wine Australia (Federal)

### 7.1 Wine Maker's Licence
- **Legislation:** *Australian Grape and Wine Authority Act 2013* (Cth)
- **Regulator:** Wine Australia
- **Requirement:** All wine producers must hold a **wine maker's licence** issued by Wine Australia. Required before making or selling wine.
- Annual licence fee based on production volume.
- **URL:** https://www.wineaustralia.com/regulation/licensing

### 7.2 Geographical Indication (GI)
- Key Tasmanian GIs: Tasmania, Coal River Valley, Derwent Valley, East Coast Tasmania, Huon Valley, Northern Tasmania, Piper's River, Tamar Valley.
- Use of a GI on a label requires ≥85% of grapes from that region.

### 7.3 Wine Equalisation Tax (WET)
- **Regulator:** Australian Taxation Office (ATO)
- WET applies at 29% of the wholesale value of wine. Wine producers may be eligible for the **WET Producer Rebate** (up to $350,000 per year for eligible small producers).
- **URL:** https://www.ato.gov.au/businesses-and-organisations/gst-excise-and-indirect-taxes/wine-equalisation-tax
