# Tasmania Agritourism Planning Notes (TPS Fact Sheet, July 2024)

## Source
State Planning Office, Department of Premier and Cabinet
URL: https://www.stateplanning.tas.gov.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0017/543122/State-Planning-Office-fact-sheet-agritourism-final-July-2024.pdf

## Key Points
- The Tasmanian Planning Scheme (TPS) created Rural and Agriculture zones in 2017
- Agritourism activities are classified into Use Classes with different assessment pathways:
  - No Permit Required
  - Permitted
  - Discretionary

## Table 1 – Summary of Allowable Agritourism Activities

| Agritourism Activity | Examples | Planning Requirements |
|---|---|---|
| Small-scale farm experiences | Farm tours, 'pick your own fruit' | Can be No Permit Required if considered to be a sideline part to an agricultural use |
| Sale of farm goods and products | Primary produce sales, shops, markets, wine tasting/cellar door sales | Permitted if associated with Resource Development (agricultural uses) or Resource Processing (e.g. winery, brewery, cidery, distillery, cheese factory) |
| Roadside stalls in the road reserve | Roadside stalls | Exempt from a planning permit if located on the edge of a council road. May be subject to council by-laws. A roadside stall located inside your land is treated as a shop. |
| Home-based business | Small scale start up activity | Permitted in the Rural Zone and Agriculture Zone |
| Dining | Cafes, restaurants | Permitted if associated with Resource Processing |

## Key Regulatory Principle
- Winery/cellar door classified as Resource Processing under TPS
- Permitted use in Rural Zone and Agriculture Zone (no discretionary assessment required for standard winery + cellar door)
- Recommend discussing with qualified planner or council before commencing
- State Policy on the Protection of Agricultural Land 2009 applies

## Legislation
- Land Use Planning and Approvals Act 1993 (LUPAA)
