# AU State Regulatory Gaps Research Notes

## WESTERN AUSTRALIA (WA)

### Wastewater / Environmental
- Act: Environmental Protection Act 1986 (WA)
- Administered by: Department of Water and Environmental Regulation (DWER)
- Wineries producing ≥350 kL of wine per year (~500 tonnes) are a **prescribed premise** under the EP Act
- Prescribed premises require an **Environmental Protection Licence** from DWER
- Wastewater irrigation: volumes exceeding 100 kL/year may require a licence and Environmental Impact Assessment (Part V)
- Effluent discharge between 200–500 kL/year depending on water use efficiency
- Effluent must be treated to minimise risk to soil and groundwater
- Reference: WA Government Water Quality Protection Note 73 (Wineries and Distilleries)
- URL: https://www.legislation.wa.gov.au/legislation/statutes.nsf/main_mrtitle_304_homepage.html
- DWER URL: https://www.dwer.wa.gov.au/

### WHS
- Act: Work Health and Safety Act 2020 (WA) — WA adopted the Model WHS Act in 2020 (later than other states)
- Administered by: WorkSafe WA
- Same obligations as other states: PCBU duty to eliminate/minimise risks
- Confined spaces (CO2 during fermentation), hazardous substances (SO2), manual handling key winery risks
- URL: https://www.legislation.wa.gov.au/legislation/statutes.nsf/main_mrtitle_14286_homepage.html
- WorkSafe WA: https://www.worksafe.wa.gov.au/

### Planning
- Act: Planning and Development Act 2005 (WA)
- Administered by: Western Australian Planning Commission (WAPC) and local councils
- New winery buildings or expansions in rural/agricultural zones typically require a **development approval** from local council
- Margaret River and Swan Valley regions have specific local planning policies for wineries
- Cellar doors in rural zones may require a change of use approval
- URL: https://www.legislation.wa.gov.au/legislation/statutes.nsf/main_mrtitle_1329_homepage.html

### Water Licensing
- Act: Rights in Water and Irrigation Act 1914 (WA)
- Administered by: DWER
- Extraction of groundwater or surface water for irrigation requires a **water licence**
- URL: https://www.legislation.wa.gov.au/legislation/statutes.nsf/main_mrtitle_1785_homepage.html

## QUEENSLAND (QLD)

### Wastewater / Environmental
- Act: Environmental Protection Act 1994 (QLD)
- Administered by: Department of Environment and Science (DES)
- Wineries are an **environmentally relevant activity (ERA)** if they produce ≥100 kL/year of wine
- ERA requires an **environmental authority (EA)** from DES
- Wastewater must be managed to prevent contamination of waterways and groundwater
- Irrigation of wastewater must comply with soil application rates
- URL: https://www.legislation.qld.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-1994-062
- DES URL: https://environment.des.qld.gov.au/

### WHS
- Act: Work Health and Safety Act 2011 (QLD)
- Administered by: Workplace Health and Safety Queensland (WHSQ)
- Adopted Model WHS Act — same obligations as federal framework
- Confined spaces, hazardous chemicals (SO2, CO2), manual handling key winery risks
- URL: https://www.legislation.qld.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-2011-018
- WHSQ URL: https://www.worksafe.qld.gov.au/

### Planning
- Act: Planning Act 2016 (QLD)
- Administered by: Department of State Development, Infrastructure, Local Government and Planning; local councils
- New winery buildings in rural zones require a **development application (DA)** to local council
- Cellar doors may require a material change of use approval
- Granite Belt and Scenic Rim regions have specific planning overlays for wineries
- URL: https://www.legislation.qld.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-2016-025

### Water Licensing
- Act: Water Act 2000 (QLD)
- Administered by: Department of Regional Development, Manufacturing and Water
- Extraction of surface or groundwater requires a **water licence** or **water allocation**
- URL: https://www.legislation.qld.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-2000-034

## TASMANIA (TAS)

### Wastewater / Environmental
- Act: Environmental Management and Pollution Control Act 1994 (TAS) — EMPCA
- Administered by: Environment Protection Authority Tasmania (EPA TAS)
- Wineries are a **level 2 activity** under EMPCA if they process ≥100 tonnes of grapes/year
- Level 2 activities require an **environmental licence** from EPA TAS
- Wastewater must be managed to prevent contamination of waterways, groundwater, and soil
- Irrigation of wastewater must comply with EPA TAS guidelines
- URL: https://www.legislation.tas.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-1994-028
- EPA TAS URL: https://epa.tas.gov.au/

### WHS
- Act: Work Health and Safety Act 2012 (TAS)
- Administered by: WorkSafe Tasmania
- Adopted Model WHS Act — same obligations as federal framework
- Confined spaces (CO2), hazardous substances (SO2), manual handling key winery risks
- URL: https://www.legislation.tas.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-2012-001
- WorkSafe TAS URL: https://worksafe.tas.gov.au/

### Planning
- Act: Land Use Planning and Approvals Act 1993 (TAS) — being replaced by Tasmanian Planning Scheme
- Administered by: local councils and Tasmanian Planning Commission
- New winery buildings or cellar doors in rural zones require a **development application** to local council
- Tasmanian Planning Scheme (TPS) applies from 2022 — rural resource zone typically permits wineries as permitted or discretionary use
- Derwent Valley, Huon Valley, and Coal River Valley regions have specific planning considerations
- URL: https://www.legislation.tas.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-1993-070
- TPC URL: https://www.tpc.tas.gov.au/

### Water Licensing
- Act: Water Management Act 1999 (TAS)
- Administered by: Department of Natural Resources and Environment Tasmania (NRE TAS)
- Extraction of surface or groundwater requires a **water licence**
- URL: https://www.legislation.tas.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-1999-101

## ADDITIONAL GAPS — VIC, NSW, SA

### VIC Planning (gap)
- Act: Planning and Environment Act 1987 (VIC)
- Administered by: Department of Transport and Planning; local councils
- New winery buildings in farming zones require a **planning permit** from local council
- Cellar doors in farming zones typically require a planning permit for change of use
- Yarra Valley, Mornington Peninsula, and Heathcote regions have specific local planning policies
- URL: https://www.legislation.vic.gov.au/in-force/acts/planning-and-environment-act-1987

### NSW Planning (gap)
- Act: Environmental Planning and Assessment Act 1979 (NSW) — EP&A Act
- Administered by: Department of Planning, Housing and Infrastructure; local councils
- New winery buildings in rural zones require a **development application (DA)** to local council
- Cellar doors may require a change of use consent
- Hunter Valley, Orange, and Mudgee regions have specific LEP provisions for wineries
- URL: https://www.legislation.nsw.gov.au/view/html/inforce/current/act-1979-203

### SA Planning (gap)
- Act: Planning, Development and Infrastructure Act 2016 (SA) — PDI Act
- Administered by: State Planning Commission and local councils
- New winery buildings in primary production zones require a **development approval**
- Barossa Valley, Clare Valley, and McLaren Vale regions have specific planning policies
- URL: https://www.legislation.sa.gov.au/lz/c/a/planning,%20development%20and%20infrastructure%20act%202016.aspx
