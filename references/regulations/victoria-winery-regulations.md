# Victoria — Winery Regulatory Requirements

> Compiled for boutique winery operators in Victoria, Australia.
> Last updated: May 2026. Always verify current requirements with the relevant authority.

---

## 1. Liquor Licensing — Liquor Control Victoria (LCV)

### Governing Legislation
**Liquor Control Reform Act 1998 (Vic)**

### Licence Type: Producer's Liquor Licence
The Producer's Liquor Licence is the standard licence for Victorian wineries. It is administered by **Liquor Control Victoria (LCV)**, a division of the Department of Justice and Community Safety.

**What it authorises:**
- Selling wine (and other alcohol you produce) retail or wholesale
- Selling your alcohol for on-premises consumption (cellar door) or takeaway
- Selling wholesale to other licensees anywhere, anytime
- Home delivery (phone, email, online or app orders)
- Selling at promotional events (farmers' markets, festivals) between 7 am and 8 pm — records of events must be kept
- A second retail location in the same wine region selling packaged alcohol

**Trading hours (retail/cellar door):**
- Monday to Saturday: 7 am to 11 pm (except Good Friday and Anzac Day)
- Sunday, Good Friday and Anzac Day: 10 am to 11 pm

**Application fees (non-refundable):**
- Floor space 0–150 m²: $334
- Floor space >150 m²: $504.30

**Annual renewal fees** apply separately.

**Processing time:** Allow at least 11 weeks. Applications are referred to Victoria Police and the relevant local council, both of which may object.

**Contact:** Liquor Control Victoria — [www.vic.gov.au/apply-producers-liquor-licence](https://www.vic.gov.au/apply-producers-liquor-licence)

### Responsible Service of Alcohol (RSA)
All staff involved in selling, serving or supplying alcohol must hold a current RSA certificate from an approved training provider.

## 2. Environmental Regulation — EPA Victoria

### Governing Legislation
**Environment Protection Act 2017 (Vic)** and **Environment Protection Regulations 2021 (Vic)**

### D09 — Beverage Manufacturing Licence

Under Schedule 1 of the Environment Protection Regulations 2021, beverage manufacturing is a **prescribed activity** (category D09) requiring EPA permission.

**Boutique winery exemption:**
A winery is **exempt from the D09 operating licence** if it meets **both** of the following conditions:
1. Processes **less than 300 tonnes of grapes per year**, AND
2. Discharges or deposits all waste **solely to land** (no discharge to waterways or sewer)

> **Important:** Even if exempt from the operating licence, a winery may still require a **D09 development licence** before constructing or significantly modifying winery infrastructure.

**If you exceed 300 tonnes/year or discharge to water/sewer:**
Both a development licence and an operating licence are required from EPA Victoria.

**Wastewater management:**
- Winery wastewater (marc, lees, wash water, grape juice) has high BOD and must be managed carefully
- Land application of wastewater is the most common approach for small wineries
- An **A14 — Reclaimed Wastewater Supply or Use** permit may be required if reclaimed wastewater is used on-farm

**EPA Victoria contact:** 1300 EPA VIC (1300 372 842)
**Online portal:** [www.epa.vic.gov.au/d09-beverage-manufacturing](https://www.epa.vic.gov.au/d09-beverage-manufacturing)

## 3. Workplace Health and Safety — WorkSafe Victoria

### Governing Legislation
**Occupational Health and Safety Act 2004 (Vic)** and **Occupational Health and Safety Regulations 2017 (Vic)**

### Key Obligations
- Provide and maintain a working environment that is safe and without risks to health
- Identify and manage hazards: cellar equipment (pumps, presses, crushers), confined spaces (tanks, fermenters), chemicals (SO₂, caustic cleaning agents, CO₂ build-up during fermentation)
- Maintain plant and equipment to manufacturer specifications
- Provide adequate training, instruction and supervision
- Keep records of hazard identification and risk assessments

### Specific Winery Hazards
- **CO₂ accumulation** in fermentation areas — ventilation and gas monitoring required
- **Confined space entry** (tanks, vats) — permit-to-work system required
- **Manual handling** — crush/harvest season involves heavy lifting; risk assessments required
- **Electrical safety** — wet cellar environments require appropriate electrical installations
- **Chemical handling** — SO₂ (sulfur dioxide) and caustic cleaning agents require SDS, PPE and training

**Contact:** WorkSafe Victoria — 1800 136 089 — [www.worksafe.vic.gov.au](https://www.worksafe.vic.gov.au)

## 4. Planning and Land Use — Local Council / DELWP

### Governing Legislation
**Planning and Environment Act 1987 (Vic)**

### Planning Permits
A **planning permit** from the relevant local council (Shire or City Council) is required for:
- Establishing a new winery on agricultural land
- Constructing or expanding cellar door facilities
- Operating a cellar door restaurant or function venue
- Significant changes to existing buildings or use

**Farming Zone (FZ) considerations:**
Most vineyard land is in the Farming Zone. A winery is generally a "use" requiring a permit in the FZ. Cellar door sales and hospitality uses typically require a separate permit and may be subject to conditions on hours, patron numbers, and signage.

**Contact:** Your local council's planning department. The relevant planning scheme is available at [www.planning.vic.gov.au](https://www.planning.vic.gov.au).

## 5. Water Licensing — Southern Rural Water / GMW / Local Catchment Management Authority

### Governing Legislation
**Water Act 1989 (Vic)**

### Water Licences
- A **water licence** (take and use licence) is required to extract water from a waterway, bore or aquifer for winery or vineyard use
- Licences are issued by the relevant **rural water corporation** (e.g., Southern Rural Water, Goulburn-Murray Water) or the **local catchment management authority**
- Water use must be metered and reported annually
- **Stormwater and wastewater** must not be discharged to waterways without EPA permission

**Contact:** Southern Rural Water — [www.srw.com.au](https://www.srw.com.au) | Goulburn-Murray Water — [www.gmwater.com.au](https://www.gmwater.com.au)

## 6. Food Safety — Local Council / Department of Health

### Governing Legislation
**Food Act 1984 (Vic)** and **Food Standards Australia New Zealand (FSANZ) Food Standards Code**

### Registration
- Wineries that sell wine for consumption (including cellar door) are classified as **food businesses** under the Food Act 1984
- Registration with the **local council** is required
- Compliance with the FSANZ Food Standards Code is mandatory (Standard 4.5 — Wine Production Requirements)

**Contact:** Your local council's environmental health officer.

## 7. Federal Obligations (also applicable in Victoria)

Victorian winery operators must also comply with all federal regulatory requirements:

| Obligation | Authority | Key Requirement |
|---|---|---|
| Wine Equalisation Tax (WET) | ATO | Register if annual wine sales >$75,000; 29% WET applies |
| Label Integrity Program (LIP) | Wine Australia | Vintage, variety and region claims must be substantiated |
| Wine Australia registration | Wine Australia | Required for export; domestic producers should register |
| Excise (spirits only) | ATO | Excise manufacturer licence required if producing spirits |
| Biosecurity | DAFF | Phytosanitary requirements for vine material imports |

See the federal regulations document for full detail: `federal/australian-winery-federal-regulations.md`

## 8. Summary Checklist for a New Boutique Winery in Victoria

| Step | Requirement | Authority |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Check local planning scheme; obtain planning permit | Local Council |
| 2 | Apply for Producer's Liquor Licence | Liquor Control Victoria |
| 3 | Confirm EPA D09 licence status (exempt if <300t/yr, waste to land) | EPA Victoria |
| 4 | Apply for D09 development licence if constructing new infrastructure | EPA Victoria |
| 5 | Obtain water take-and-use licence | Rural Water Corporation |
| 6 | Register as a food business | Local Council |
| 7 | Ensure WHS compliance (confined spaces, CO₂, chemicals) | WorkSafe Victoria |
| 8 | Register for WET with ATO (if sales >$75,000) | ATO |
| 9 | Register with Wine Australia | Wine Australia |
| 10 | Ensure all staff hold current RSA certificates | LCV / Training Provider |
