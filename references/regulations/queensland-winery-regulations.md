# Queensland Winery Regulatory Requirements

**Jurisdiction:** Queensland, Australia
**Last reviewed:** May 2026
**Prepared for:** Ownology Cellar Intelligence Platform

---

## 1. Liquor & Wine Licensing — Office of Liquor and Gaming Regulation (OLGR)

### Primary Legislation
- **Wine Industry Act 1994 (Qld)**
- **Wine Industry Regulation 2009 (Qld)**
- **Liquor Act 1992 (Qld)**

### Wine Producer Licence
A **wine producer licence** is required to operate a vineyard or winery in Queensland. It is issued by the **Office of Liquor and Gaming Regulation (OLGR)**, which sits within the Department of Justice.

**What the licence allows:**
- Tasting and on-premises consumption of wine produced on-site
- Takeaway (off-premises) sales of wine
- Sales at satellite cellar doors (with separate approval)
- Sale of other producers' wine on-premises, provided it does not exceed 49% of annual sales

**Approved Wine Nominee:**
An approved wine nominee must be present on the licensed premises. If you hold more than one licence, a separate wine nominee is required for each premises.

**Annual obligations:**
- Annual licence fees due by **31 July** each year (payable via OLGR client portal)
- Annual return lodged online via the Liquor, Gaming and Fair Trading portal, listing wine produced, sold, purchased, and blended

**Application process:**
- Form 1: Application for a wine producer licence
- Form 5: Personal details schedule (wine)
- Criminal history and probity checks required
- Advertising of application may be required
- Processing time: approximately **2–3 months**

**Satellite Cellar Door:**
Wine producer licensees may apply for satellite cellar door approval (Form 10) to sell wine at a separate premises. The satellite cellar door must have a nominated adult wine nominee.

**Key contacts:**
- OLGR: 13 QGOV (13 74 68)
- OLGR client portal: https://secure.olgr.qld.gov.au/portal/
- ABLIS listing: https://ablis.business.gov.au/service/qld/wine-producer-licence/4239

## 2. Environmental Regulation — Department of the Environment, Tourism, Science and Innovation (DETSI)

### Primary Legislation
- **Environmental Protection Act 1994 (Qld)**
- **Environmental Protection Regulation 2019 (Qld)**

### Environmentally Relevant Activity (ERA) — ERA 22: Beverage Production
Wineries producing **1 megalitre (1,000 kL) or more** of alcoholic beverages per year are subject to **ERA 22(2) — Beverage Production** under Schedule 2 of the Environmental Protection Regulation 2019.

An **Environmental Authority (EA)** must be obtained before commencing this activity. Operating without an EA is an offence under section 426 of the Environmental Protection Act 1994.

**Annual fee (ERA 22(2)):**
- Aggregate Environmental Score (AES) = 55
- Annual fee (2025): approximately **$17,281**
- Fee is calculated by multiplying the fee unit by the AES

**Application pathway:**
- Standard application if eligibility criteria are met
- Site-specific application for higher-risk or non-standard operations
- Applications lodged via DETSI Online Services
- Contact: PALM (Permit and Licence Management) — palm@detsi.qld.gov.au

**Wastewater and effluent:**
Winery wastewater (grape marc, wash water, fermentation residues) must be managed in accordance with EA conditions. Discharge to waterways or land is regulated. Wineries should also consider ERA 23 (Bottling or canning 200 tonnes or more of food) if applicable.

**Key contacts:**
- DETSI: https://www.detsi.qld.gov.au/
- ERA fees page: https://www.detsi.qld.gov.au/our-department/fees-services?item_id=35744

## 3. Workplace Health & Safety — WorkSafe Queensland

### Primary Legislation
- **Work Health and Safety Act 2011 (Qld)**
- **Work Health and Safety Regulation 2011 (Qld)**

### Obligations
Under the WHS Act 2011, a person conducting a business or undertaking (PCBU) must ensure, so far as is reasonably practicable, the health and safety of workers and others at the workplace.

**Key winery WHS obligations:**
- Safe systems of work for cellar operations (confined spaces, chemical handling, forklift use)
- Hazardous chemicals register and safety data sheets (SDS) for SO₂, cleaning agents, acids
- Confined space entry procedures for tanks, vats, and fermentation vessels
- Manual handling risk assessments for barrel handling and bottling operations
- Noise exposure assessments for bottling lines and machinery
- First aid facilities and emergency response plans
- Workers' Compensation insurance (WorkCover Queensland)

**Incident notification:**
Serious incidents (death, serious injury, dangerous incidents) must be notified to WorkSafe Queensland immediately.

**Key contacts:**
- WorkSafe Queensland: 1300 362 128
- Website: https://www.worksafe.qld.gov.au/

## 4. Planning & Development Approval — Local Government / State Assessment

### Primary Legislation
- **Planning Act 2016 (Qld)**
- **Planning Regulation 2017 (Qld)**
- Local government planning schemes

### Development Approval
Establishing or expanding a winery, cellar door, or associated tourism facilities generally requires a **development approval (DA)** from the relevant local government under the Planning Act 2016.

**Key considerations:**
- Wineries are typically located in **rural zones** under local planning schemes
- A winery (as a rural industry or food and drink outlet) may be **accepted development** in a rural zone, or may require **code assessment** or **impact assessment** depending on the local scheme
- Cellar doors with on-site dining, events, or accommodation may require separate approvals
- State Planning Policy (Agriculture) supports compatible land uses such as wineries, rural industry, and agritourism on agricultural land
- The **Planning Regulation 2017** sets out assessment benchmarks for development in rural zones

**Agritourism:**
Queensland's planning framework supports agritourism activities (including cellar doors, farm stays, and farm tours) in rural zones. Local planning schemes may include specific agritourism provisions.

**Key contacts:**
- Queensland Planning: https://www.planning.qld.gov.au/
- Local government planning department (varies by region)

## 5. Water Licensing — Department of Regional Development, Manufacturing and Water

### Primary Legislation
- **Water Act 2000 (Qld)**
- **Water Regulation 2016 (Qld)**

### Water Licence Requirements
Under the Water Act 2000, a **water licence** is required for:
- Taking or interfering with surface water (watercourse, lake, or spring) for irrigation, industrial, or commercial use
- Taking underground water in groundwater areas established under the Water Regulation 2016 or a water plan
- Overland flow water in areas identified in moratorium notices or water plans

**Key requirements:**
- Water licences state either a maximum annual volume or a maximum irrigable area
- Exceeding licensed limits is an offence under the Water Act 2000
- Water use is subject to routine property audits
- Water licences attach to land and may only be used on the land to which they are attached

**Application process:**
- Apply via local business centre or online
- Applications must comply with relevant water plans for the region

**Key contacts:**
- Business Queensland water licences: https://www.business.qld.gov.au/industries/mining-energy-water/water/authorisations/licences/requirements
- 13 QGOV (13 74 68)

## 6. Food Safety — Local Council (Food Act 2006)

### Primary Legislation
- **Food Act 2006 (Qld)**
- **Food Production (Safety) Act 2000 (Qld)**
- **Australia New Zealand Food Standards Code**

### Food Business Licence
Under the Food Act 2006, most food businesses must obtain a **food business licence from their local council**.

**Winery-specific considerations:**
- The sale of alcoholic drinks (including wine) is **exempt** from the food business licence requirement under the Food Act 2006 — the sale of drinks (other than fruit or vegetable juice processed at the place of sale) is listed as an exemption
- However, if the cellar door also **manufactures food** (e.g., cheeseboards, prepared meals, charcuterie) or **sells unpackaged food by retail**, a food business licence from the local council is required
- A **Food Safety Supervisor** certificate is required for Category 1 and Category 2 food businesses

**Food safety compliance:**
Even if a food business licence is not required, all food handling must comply with the Australia New Zealand Food Standards Code and the Food Act 2006.

**Key contacts:**
- Queensland Health food business licences: https://www.qld.gov.au/health/staying-healthy/food-pantry/starting-a-food-business/food-business-licences/do-i-need-a-food-business-licence
- Local council (varies by region)

## Key Queensland Wine Regions

Queensland's wine regions include:
- **Granite Belt** (Stanthorpe/Ballandean) — Queensland's premier wine region, high altitude, cool climate
- **South Burnett** — subtropical viticulture, Kingaroy area
- **Scenic Rim** — emerging region near Beaudesert
