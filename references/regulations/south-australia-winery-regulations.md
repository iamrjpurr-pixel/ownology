# South Australia — Winery Regulatory and Legislative Requirements

*Compiled May 2026 | Sources: CBS SA, EPA SA, SafeWork SA, SA.GOV.AU, Landscape SA*

---

## Overview

South Australia produces approximately 80% of Australia's premium wine across more than 700 wineries. Operating a winery in SA requires compliance with a layered regulatory framework spanning liquor licensing, environmental protection, planning and development, work health and safety, water allocation, and food safety. This document covers each layer, the relevant legislation, the administering agency, and the key obligations for boutique producers.

---

## 1. Liquor Licensing

**Administering Agency:** Consumer and Business Services (CBS), Attorney-General's Department
**Key Legislation:** *Liquor Licensing Act 1997* (SA)
**Portal:** Liquor and Gaming Online (LGO) — https://www.cbs.sa.gov.au

### Licence Type for Wineries

Wineries require a **Producer's Licence**, which authorises the production and sale of the licensee's own product. There are three classes:

| Class | Description | Trading |
|---|---|---|
| **Class 1** | Producer — consumption off premises only | Wholesale and direct sales only; no on-site consumption |
| **Class 2** | Producer — cellar door (consumption on and off) | Cellar door tastings and sales; on-site consumption permitted |
| **Class 3** | Producer — full retail and on-site consumption | Broadest permissions; includes restaurant/dining |

Most boutique wineries operating a cellar door require a **Class 2 Producer's Licence**.

### Application Requirements

To apply for a Producer's Licence, the applicant must provide:

- Completed application form via LGO portal
- Proof of production premises (lease or title)
- Floor plan of the premises
- Council approval (development consent) for the use
- Evidence of production capacity and intent
- Responsible Service of Alcohol (RSA) certification for all staff serving alcohol
- Payment of application fee (varies by class; Class 2 approximately $1,000–$2,500 for initial application)

### Ongoing Compliance Obligations

- Annual licence renewal with fee payment
- Compliance with trading hours conditions (cellar doors typically 9am–5pm or as specified)
- Responsible Service of Alcohol for all staff
- Minors policy (Class 2 may permit minors in cellar door areas with conditions)
- Record-keeping of sales volumes
- Compliance with the *Liquor Licensing General Code of Practice*
- Notification to CBS of any change in premises, ownership, or trading conditions

### Wine Export Licence

Wineries exporting wine from SA also require a separate **Wine Export Licence** from Wine Australia (federal), not from CBS. See the federal regulatory document for details.

## 2. Environmental Protection (EPA)

**Administering Agency:** Environment Protection Authority (EPA SA)
**Key Legislation:** *Environment Protection Act 1993* (SA); *Environment Protection Regulations 2023*
**Key Document:** *EPA Guidelines for Wineries and Distilleries* (Revised April 2017)

### EPA Licence Threshold

An EPA licence is **mandatory** for wineries that:

- Process **more than 50 tonnes** of grape or grape product per annum within the **Mount Lofty Ranges Watershed Protection Area** (declared under Part 8 of the *Environment Protection Act 1993*), OR
- Process **more than 500 tonnes** elsewhere in the state.

Boutique wineries below these thresholds are not required to hold an EPA licence but are still subject to the general environmental duty under Section 25 of the Act, which requires all reasonable and practicable measures to prevent or minimise environmental harm.

### General Environmental Duty (Section 25)

All wineries — licensed or not — must:

- Prevent or minimise pollution of water, soil, and air
- Manage wastewater, marc, lees, and other solid waste appropriately
- Control odour from open lagoons, composting marc, or wastewater treatment
- Manage noise from pumps, chillers, crushers, and vintage traffic

### Licensed Winery Obligations

Licensed wineries must:

- Develop and implement an **Environmental Monitoring Program** approved by the EPA
- Monitor influent water, wastewater, soil, groundwater, and receiving environments
- Use **NATA-accredited laboratories** for all required analyses
- Submit monitoring data to the EPA **annually**
- Engage an **independent qualified verifier** to audit the monitoring program at intervals prescribed by the licence
- Measure wastewater flow volumes using calibrated flow meters
- Maintain calibration records available for inspection at any time

### Wastewater Management

Winery wastewater is generated from tank cleaning, barrel washing, floor washing, press operations, and bottling line cleaning. Key requirements:

- Wastewater must be collected and treated before disposal or reuse
- Discharge to land (irrigation) must comply with EPA nutrient loading guidelines
- Discharge to sewer or STEDS requires compliance with SA Water trade waste requirements
- Monthly wastewater generation data must be reported to the EPA annually (even for sewer-connected facilities)

### Key Wastewater Parameters Monitored

pH, BOD, COD, TSS, EC (salinity), total nitrogen, total phosphorus, total potassium, SAR (sodicity), and heavy metals.

### Solid Waste

- Marc (grape skins/seeds) must be managed to prevent odour and leachate
- Lees and filter solids must be disposed of appropriately (composting, land application, or licensed waste facility)
- Wastewater treatment sludge requires monitoring for heavy metals before land application

### Environmental Management Plan

The EPA and Green Industries SA recommend all wineries (licensed or not) develop a written **Environmental Management Plan** covering: water use, wastewater management, solid waste, energy use, chemical storage, and emergency response.

## 3. Planning and Development Approval

**Administering Agency:** Local Council (Development Assessment) + State Planning Commission (for significant developments)
**Key Legislation:** *Planning, Development and Infrastructure Act 2016* (SA); *Planning and Design Code 2026*

### When Approval is Required

Development approval is required for:

- Establishing a new winery on rural or agricultural land
- Constructing new buildings (processing shed, barrel hall, bottling facility)
- Establishing or expanding a cellar door
- Adding accommodation, restaurant, or events facilities
- Significant change of use of existing buildings

### Planning and Design Code — Rural Zone

Most SA wineries operate in the **Rural Zone** or **Primary Production Zone** under the Planning and Design Code. Key considerations:

- Winery processing facilities are generally **accepted development** in primary production zones where they are ancillary to viticulture on the same land
- Cellar doors are typically **performance assessed** (require development application and assessment against Code criteria)
- Restaurants, accommodation, and events facilities are **performance assessed** and may require additional traffic, noise, and amenity assessments
- Development within the **Mount Lofty Ranges** may be subject to additional overlay controls (bushfire, water catchment, scenic landscape)

### Application Process

1. Lodge development application with the relevant local council (e.g., Barossa Council, Adelaide Hills Council, McLaren Vale/Onkaparinga Council)
2. Council assesses against the Planning and Design Code
3. Referrals may be required to: EPA SA, SA Water, DPTI, Heritage SA (if heritage overlay applies)
4. Public notification may be required for performance-assessed developments
5. Development approval issued with conditions (e.g., operating hours, car parking, landscaping, stormwater management)

### Key Councils for SA Wine Regions

|---|---|

| Eden Valley | Barossa Council / Clare & Gilbert Valleys |
| McLaren Vale | City of Onkaparinga |

## 4. Work Health and Safety (WHS)

**Administering Agency:** SafeWork SA
**Key Legislation:** *Work Health and Safety Act 2012* (SA); *Work Health and Safety Regulations 2012* (SA)
**Key Resource:** SafeWork SA — Vineyards & Wine industry page

### General Obligations

All wineries are **Persons Conducting a Business or Undertaking (PCBUs)** under the WHS Act and must:

- Provide and maintain a safe work environment, plant, and systems of work
- Provide adequate information, training, instruction, and supervision
- Consult with workers on health and safety matters
- Develop and maintain emergency plans
- Ensure contractors and labour hire workers are covered by appropriate safety management

### Confined Spaces — Critical Obligation

**Wine tanks, fermentation vessels, and underground cellars are classified as confined spaces** under the WHS Regulations. This is the single most significant WHS obligation for winery operators.

Requirements:

- **Confined space entry permit** must be issued before any worker enters a tank or vessel
- Risk assessment must be conducted before each entry
- Atmospheric testing for CO₂ and O₂ levels must be performed before and during entry
- Standby person must be present outside the confined space during entry
- Rescue equipment and emergency procedures must be in place
- Workers must be trained in confined space entry procedures
- CO₂ fixed-point detectors are strongly recommended in fermentation areas (alarm thresholds: 5,000 ppm TWA; 30,000 ppm STEL)

### Hazardous Chemicals

- SO₂ (sulphur dioxide) is a hazardous chemical requiring a Safety Data Sheet (SDS) and safe handling procedures
- Caustic cleaning agents (NaOH, peracetic acid) require SDS, PPE, and spill containment
- Tartaric acid, DAP, and other additions require SDS on file
- Chemical storage must comply with the *Dangerous Goods Act 1979* (SA) where quantities exceed threshold limits

### Manual Handling

- Barrel handling (225L oak barrel = ~290kg full) is a significant manual handling risk
- Mechanical handling aids (barrel trolleys, forklifts, barrel washers) are required for regular barrel operations
- Forklift operators require a High Risk Work Licence (HRWL) — Forklift (LF class)

### Electrical Safety

- 3-phase electrical installations must be installed and maintained by a licensed electrician
- Residual current devices (RCDs) required on all circuits in wet areas
- Annual electrical safety testing recommended

### Seasonal and Casual Workers

- Seasonal harvest workers (including backpackers on working holiday visas) must receive WHS induction
- Emergency procedures must be communicated to all workers including those with limited English
- Adequate amenities (toilets, drinking water, first aid) must be provided

## 5. Water Allocation and Licensing

**Administering Agency:** Department for Environment and Water (DEW); Landscape SA Boards
**Key Legislation:** *Landscape South Australia Act 2019*; Water Allocation Plans (region-specific)

### When a Water Licence is Required

A water licence is required to take water from:

- A watercourse (river, creek, drain)
- A lake or reservoir
- A well or bore (groundwater)

Rainwater collected from roofs and stored in tanks on the property does not generally require a licence.

### Water Allocation Plans

Water use in SA wine regions is governed by **Water Allocation Plans** administered by the relevant Landscape SA Board. Key plans affecting wine regions:

| Region | Water Allocation Plan |
|---|---|
| Barossa | Barossa Prescribed Water Resources Area |
| McLaren Vale | McLaren Vale Prescribed Wells Area |
| Adelaide Hills | Mount Lofty Ranges Prescribed Water Resources Area |
| Clare Valley | Clare Valley Prescribed Water Resources Area |
| Coonawarra | Tatiara Prescribed Wells Area |
| Riverland | River Murray Prescribed Watercourse |

### Key Obligations

- Water licences specify the annual volume allocation (megalitres)
- Metering is required for all licensed water takes
- Annual meter reads must be submitted to DEW
- Water trading (buying/selling allocations) is permitted in most prescribed areas
- Exceeding allocation triggers compliance action and potential penalties

## 6. Food Safety

**Administering Agency:** SA Health / FSANZ (federal standard applies)
**Key Legislation:** *Food Act 2001* (SA); *Food Standards Code* — Standard 4.5.1 (Wine Production Requirements)

Wine production in SA is subject to the same federal food safety standards as all Australian states. Key SA-specific requirements:

- Wineries are classified as **food businesses** under the *Food Act 2001* and must notify SA Health of their food business activity
- Food safety programs are not mandatory for most wine producers (wine is a low-risk food category) but are recommended
- Premises must be maintained in a clean and sanitary condition
- Pest control programs must be in place
- Temperature control for wine storage is not legislated but is a quality and contractual obligation

## 7. Biosecurity

**Administering Agency:** Primary Industries and Regions SA (PIRSA)
**Key Legislation:** *Plant Health Act 2009* (SA); *Phylloxera and Grape Industry Act 1995* (SA)

### Phylloxera

South Australia (excluding the Riverland) is a **phylloxera exclusion zone**. Movement of soil, plant material, and equipment into SA wine regions from phylloxera-affected states (VIC, NSW, ACT) is strictly controlled.

Key obligations:

- All equipment (harvesters, tractors, bins) entering SA from affected states must be cleaned and inspected
- Planting material must be sourced from accredited nurseries
- Any suspected phylloxera detection must be reported to PIRSA immediately
- Penalties for non-compliance are severe (up to $500,000 for corporations)

### Exotic Pests and Diseases

- Exotic vine diseases (e.g., Xylella fastidiosa, Grapevine Flavescence Dorée) are notifiable under the *Plant Health Act 2009*
- Suspected detections must be reported to the PIRSA Emergency Plant Pest Hotline: 1800 084 881

## 8. Noise and Amenity

**Administering Agency:** EPA SA; Local Council
**Key Legislation:** *Environment Protection Act 1993* — Environment Protection (Noise) Policy 2007

- Winery operations (pumps, chillers, crushers, vehicle movements) must comply with the **Environment Protection (Noise) Policy 2007**
- Noise limits apply at the nearest sensitive receiver (residential property boundary)
- Vintage operations (24-hour crushing during harvest) may require a noise management plan submitted to the EPA
- Cellar door events and amplified music may require council approval and noise management conditions

## 10. Regulatory Compliance Checklist for SA Boutique Wineries

| Obligation | Threshold | Agency | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Producer's Licence (Class 2) | All cellar door operators | CBS | Annual renewal |
| RSA certification — all staff | All licensed premises | CBS | On engagement |
| EPA licence | >50t (Mt Lofty) / >500t elsewhere | EPA SA | 5-year term |
| Environmental monitoring program | Licensed wineries | EPA SA | Annual data submission |
| Development approval | New buildings, cellar door, change of use | Local Council | Per development |
| Confined space entry permit | All tank entries | SafeWork SA | Per entry |
| CO₂ monitoring | Fermentation areas | SafeWork SA | Continuous (vintage) |
| Water licence and metering | All licensed water takes | DEW / Landscape SA | Annual meter read |
| Food business notification | All food businesses | SA Health | On establishment |
| Phylloxera compliance | All SA wine regions | PIRSA | Ongoing |
| WHS induction — seasonal workers | All seasonal staff | SafeWork SA | On engagement |
