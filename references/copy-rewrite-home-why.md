## Brand Voice Principles (applies to both pages)

The winemaker is always the expert. Ownology is always the apprentice. The intelligence is theirs — accumulated over every vintage, every decision, every hard lesson. Ownology holds it, organises it, and makes it available at 1am when the bandwidth is gone.

**I.A.** does not stand for Artificial Intelligence here. It stands for:

- **Artisan Intelligence** — craft knowledge, not corporate automation
- **Always Informed** — never caught without an answer
- **Awake at 1am** — there when harvest gets hard
- **Actual Inspiration** — the spark that makes this vintage better than the last
- **Accumulated Intelligence** — everything you know, searchable
- **Answers Instantly** — no delay, no lookup, no interruption

The cycling of these meanings is the brand mechanic. It should appear visually on the homepage hero — the letters "I.A." cycling through each expansion — and echo as section anchors throughout both pages.

## SECTION: The Three Problems (Cognitive Overload / Single Point of Failure / Lookup Problem)

**Section heading (rewrite):**

*Current:* *(implied)*
*Proposed section label:* `Why winemakers need I.A.`

**Problem 1 — Cognitive Overload (rewrite):**

*Current:* "During harvest, a winemaker makes hundreds of high-stakes decisions in six weeks — often simultaneously, often alone, often at 2am. When working memory is saturated, decision quality degrades. The knowledge is there. The bandwidth is not."

*Proposed:*
> Harvest is six weeks of high-stakes decisions made simultaneously, alone, under pressure, often at 1am. When working memory is saturated, decision quality degrades — not because the winemaker doesn't know, but because the bandwidth is gone. I.A. doesn't replace the judgment. It removes the lookup so the judgment can do its job.

**Problem 2 — Single Point of Failure (rewrite):**

*Current:* "In most boutique wineries, the depth of operational knowledge lives in one person's head. When that person is unavailable, a significant portion of the winery's accumulated intelligence goes with them. This is not a failure of documentation. It is a structural reality of small, craft-focused operations."

*Proposed:*
> In most boutique wineries, the accumulated intelligence of the operation lives in one person's head. When that person is unavailable — or eventually moves on — a significant portion of the winery's institutional knowledge goes with them. This is not a failure of documentation. It is the structural reality of craft-focused operations. Ownology makes sure that knowledge is never the single point of failure.

**Problem 3 — The Lookup Problem (rewrite):**

*Current:* "The question at 2am is not 'what kind of wine do I want to make?' It is 'what is the correct DAP addition for this tank right now?' That is a lookup problem — and lookup problems are precisely what well-designed AI systems solve, freeing the winemaker's judgment for decisions that genuinely require it."

*Proposed:*
> The question at 1am is not philosophical. It is: *"What is the correct DAP addition for this tank, right now?"* That is a lookup problem. Lookup problems are exactly what I.A. solves — so the winemaker's judgment is free for the decisions that genuinely require it.
