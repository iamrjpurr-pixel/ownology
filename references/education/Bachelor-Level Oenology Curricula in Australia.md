# Bachelor-Level Oenology Curricula in Australia

*Compiled May 2026 | Sources: University of Adelaide Calendar 2025, Charles Sturt University Handbook 2026*

---

## Overview

Two Australian universities offer dedicated bachelor-level programs in oenology and wine science: the **University of Adelaide** (Bachelor of Viticulture and Oenology, BVitOenol) and **Charles Sturt University** (Bachelor of Wine Science, BWineSci). Both are AQF Level 7 qualifications and are internationally recognised as among the most rigorous wine science degrees in the world. Understanding what these programs cover is directly relevant to Ownology's positioning: it reveals what winemakers already know deeply from formal training, and where the practical knowledge gaps are that an AI assistant can most usefully fill.

---

## 1. University of Adelaide — Bachelor of Viticulture and Oenology (BVitOenol)

### Program Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Program Code | BVITO |
| AQF Level | 7 |
| Duration | 4 years full-time |
| Total Units | 96 units (84 core + 12 elective) |
| Faculty | Faculty of Sciences, Engineering and Technology |
| Campus | North Terrace (Year 1), Waite Campus (Years 2–4), National Wine Centre |
| Note | No further intake from 2025 (program under review / transition) |

The Adelaide program is distinctive in its **dual viticulture and oenology focus** — graduates are qualified to work as either a viticulturalist or a winemaker. The first year is taught at the North Terrace campus and the National Wine Centre, grounding students in basic sciences. Years 2–4 are taught at the Waite Campus winery, with increasing emphasis on applied winemaking and viticultural science. Year 4 includes a mandatory industry placement.

### Program Learning Outcomes

Adelaide graduates are trained to: explain the role of viticulture and oenology in society; demonstrate knowledge of viticultural and oenological sciences; critically analyse problems in wine production; communicate effectively with industry and consumer audiences; and demonstrate knowledge of the regulatory frameworks relevant to the wine industry.

## 2. Charles Sturt University — Bachelor of Wine Science (BWineSci)

### Program Overview

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Program Code | 4410WS01 |
| AQF Level | 7 |
| Duration | 4 years full-time (minimum); 6 years part-time (maximum) |
| Total Credit Points | 192 (176 essential + 16 elective) |
| Faculty | Faculty of Science and Health |
| School | School of Agricultural, Environmental and Veterinary Sciences |
| Study Mode | Online (with optional on-campus intensive schools) |
| Entry Score | ATAR 60 |

The CSU program is the most flexible of the two, designed specifically for **working winery professionals** who want to formalise their knowledge while remaining employed. Its online delivery model and part-time pathway (up to 6 years) make it the dominant choice for assistant winemakers and cellar hands seeking to upskill. CSU has more than **1,000 graduates** working in the Australian and international wine industries.

### Program Learning Outcomes

CSU graduates are trained to: describe the environmental, biological, chemical, physiological and management processes affecting grape and wine quality; utilise wine production processes to plan and implement production of common wine styles; apply techniques to measure wine composition and appraise spoilage, style and efficiency; design and undertake basic wine research trials; analyse governance frameworks for the grape and wine industry; and develop plans based on sustainable agricultural management.

## 3. Comparative Analysis

| Dimension | University of Adelaide BVitOenol | CSU Bachelor of Wine Science |
|---|---|---|
| **Duration** | 4 years full-time only | 4–6 years (full-time or part-time) |
| **Delivery** | On-campus (Waite Campus winery) | Online + optional intensive schools |
| **Target student** | School leavers, full-time students | Working winery professionals |
| **Viticulture depth** | Deep (dual viticulture + oenology) | Moderate (VIT212 + supporting sciences) |
| **Oenology depth** | Deep (7 dedicated oenology subjects) | Deep (10 dedicated WSC subjects) |
| **Chemistry emphasis** | Strong (2 chemistry + biochemistry + genetics) | Strong (2 chemistry + wine chemistry subject) |
| **Microbiology** | Dedicated subject (Year 2) | Dedicated subject (MCR101 + WSC318) |
| **Sensory science** | Year 2 + Year 3 advanced | WSC217 + WSC316 advanced |
| **Engineering** | Dedicated subject (Year 3) | Dedicated subject (WSC321) |
| **Industry placement** | Mandatory (Year 4) | Industry Practice subject (AHT274) |
| **Business/management** | Via electives | Dedicated subject (AHT231) |
| **Research skills** | Via Advances in Wine Science (Year 4) | Via research trial design (learning outcomes) |
| **Regulatory training** | Embedded in learning outcomes | Embedded in learning outcomes |
| **Total qualification** | 96 units / 4 years | 192 credit points / 4–6 years |

## 4. Implications for Ownology

Understanding these curricula reveals both what winemakers know deeply and where Ownology adds the most value.

**What formally trained winemakers already know well:** the science of fermentation, basic wine chemistry, sensory evaluation principles, viticultural science, and the general regulatory framework. Ownology should not try to teach these fundamentals — it should assume competence and build on it.

**Where the knowledge gaps are:** the curricula cover principles but cannot keep pace with the operational specificity of a given vintage, a particular tank, a specific lot of grapes. The questions that arise at 2am during harvest — "my YAN is 95 ppm and my Brix is 26.4, what DAP rate do I use for this Grenache?" — are not answered by textbooks. They require the intersection of learned principles with real-time data and winery-specific context. That is precisely the gap Ownology is built to fill.

**The CSU part-time pathway is particularly relevant:** a large proportion of working winemakers are studying part-time via CSU while running a vintage. They are simultaneously learning the theory and needing to apply it under pressure. Ownology is a natural complement to this cohort — a tool that bridges the gap between the textbook and the cellar floor in real time.
