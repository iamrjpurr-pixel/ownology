## CSU — WSC115 Wine Science 1 (8 credit points)

**Abstract:** This subject provides an introduction to wine microbiology and the main chemical concepts and analyses that underpin wine production, including interactions with fermentation, composition and stability. Students will develop skills in the general chemical analysis of wine, with an emphasis on safety and fundamental analytical techniques.

**Syllabus Topics:**
- The science of winemaking
- Fermentation and microorganisms
- Control of microorganisms in winemaking and sanitation in the winery
- Chemistry in winemaking
- Winemaking control aspects
- Winemaking calculations and units of measurement
- Wine acids and acidity
- The use of sulfur dioxide during wine production
- Substances that complement the activity of sulfur dioxide
- Phenolic compounds
- Oxidation

**Learning Outcomes:**
1. Describe and analyse the fundamentals of wine microbiology and winery sanitation
2. Analyse winemaking problems and identify actions that will improve them
3. Safely perform quality control analysis procedures, and interpret and report results
4. Explain the chemical and microbiological principles involved with winemaking

**Workload:** 140–160 hours | **Duration:** 1 teaching period | **Grading:** HD/FL
